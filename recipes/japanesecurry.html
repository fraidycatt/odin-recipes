Japanese Curry

A weeknight dinner straight from Japan, utilizing curry roux blocks for simplicity and speed.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • A full packet of Vermont Curry
  • 5 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Air Fryer Pork Katsu recipe-- separate
  • Cooked Rice for serving

STEPS

  1. Heat a large pot over medium high heat and add your oil.
  2. When oil begins to shimmer, add onion and saute until you feel the vibes are right-- five to ten minutes.
  3. Add potatoes and carrots and cook, stirring occasionally, for about five more minutes.
  4. Add chicken broth and turn heat up to a boil. Reduce heat to a steady simmer and cover, letting it simmer until the vegetables are all cooked.
  5. Break apart the curry roux into blocks and drop into the broth and vegetable mixture. Stir until it's dissolved.
  6. Add soy sauce and sake and cook, stirring constantly to ensure the curry doesn't stick to the bottom of the pan, for two minutes.
  7. Place a serving of rice on one side of the plate; spoon the curry on the other side of the plate so that they meet in the center. Place freshly sliced, hot katsu across the top of both, and serve.
